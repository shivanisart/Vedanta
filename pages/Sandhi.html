Sanskrit words and meanings

  • Dharma (Fabric) Dharma, from the root *dhri* meaning "to hold," is the principle that sustains harmony and balance in the fabric of life—across the universe, society, your work, or relationships with partners, children, and others. It keeps these connections strong and thriving. In any relationship, rights and duties are two sides of the same coin: one person’s right creates another’s duty. Dharma focuses on fulfilling your responsibilities to uphold these bonds, unlike a rights-based culture where people prioritize claiming what’s owed to them. For example, a teacher’s duty to educate a student is the student’s right to learn, and a parent’s duty to care for their child is the child’s right to support—each role carries distinct obligations that sustain the relationship. Through the law of karma, by faithfully living your dharma, the rights or benefits you deserve will come to you. By embracing dharma, you weave trust and mutual support into the fabric of your relationships. Take Serena and Venus Williams, sisters and professional tennis players: on the court, each has a duty to play their best, which respects their opponent’s right to a fair match, even if it means setting aside family ties. This upholds dharma by honoring their roles as athletes, ensuring integrity and fairness in the game of tennis.
